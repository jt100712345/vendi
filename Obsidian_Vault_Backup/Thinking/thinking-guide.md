# Thinking Guide

This folder is dedicated to capturing and developing ideas, research, brainstorms, and conceptual thoughts.

**Guidelines:**
- **Capture Ideas:** Use this space for brainstorming, drafting, and exploring new concepts.
- **Research Notes:** Store summaries, key takeaways, and personal annotations from research (articles, books, discussions).
- **Interlinking:** Heavily interlink notes to create a web of interconnected ideas. This is where the true power of Obsidian shines for knowledge discovery.
- **Atomic Notes:** Strive for atomic notes—each note should cover a single idea or concept to maximize reusability and linking potential.
- **Evolution:** Notes in this folder are expected to evolve. Don't be afraid to refine, expand, or refactor them as your understanding grows.
- **Tagging:** Use descriptive tags (e.g., `#idea`, `#concept`, `#research`, `#philosophy`) to categorize different types of thoughts.
- **Reference Management:** When referencing external sources, ensure proper citations or links to the `Resources` folder.
